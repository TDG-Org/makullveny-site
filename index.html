A calm study room for your computer

Makullveny: study with calm focus.

Makullveny is a local-first desktop study assistant in active development. It opens with a simple dashboard, then gives students focused spaces for notes, tools, ambience, study music, and calmer class organization.

Private work-in-progress beta

The desktop app source is private while the project is being polished. This page gives a clear public overview without exposing the full codebase, user data, or deeper roadmap.

What it is

A student productivity app with a dashboard at the front and a beautiful notes desk inside.

Dashboard

Start from a calm home screen that points students toward the right workspace, tool, or study flow without making the app feel crowded.

Library Desk

Create books, bookshelves, and pages for classes, study journals, and projects. Notes can include typed text, drawings, imported images, and page customization.

Calm Study Atmosphere

Ambient sounds, a lofi radio, warm themes, and a cozy cabin workspace help make long study sessions feel less harsh and more intentional.

More Study Tools

Flashcards, quick references, class setup helpers, and practical student utilities are being shaped carefully while the app remains private.

Why it exists

Less context switching. More useful study time.

Students often bounce between notes, PDFs, calculators, converters, music, class pages, and search tabs. Makullveny is being built to bring more of that study flow into one calm place.

The goal is not to replace every school tool. The goal is to make the student feel more organized, less scattered, and better prepared while working.

Privacy

Local-first by default.

Notes stay local. Books, pages, drawings, and study content are stored on the user's computer unless the user chooses to export or move data.

Private work stays private. The public page is only a project overview. It does not include the private app source code or a user's study data.

Accounts are future optional work. The app is being designed so core study features can remain useful without forcing cloud sign-in.
